Triggers

Everybody has emotional triggers that can set them off and cause some extreme reaction which will not be able to understood by a bystander if they don't know that person well.

Triggers often have a long history behind them. When we get triggered, it's mostly connected to a situation in the past. They often connect to a perceived inadequacy about ourselves that is a source of pain to us.

When programming, I quite often come to points where I am stuck and even after googling the problem for a while. That usually sets me into a down mood thinking, that I'm not cut out to think like a programmer.

And it's worse when you ask somebody else for help and they spot the solution to my problem within seconds... That just winds me up and triggers a rather angry and unhappy response.

The Siberian North Railroad exercise

Emotional triggers can bring out the worst in everyone of us, if we let them. Self-regulation through the Siberian North Railroad exercise is a skill that will help us respond better to these triggers.

The Siberian North Railroad exercise is a useful practice for triggers and also for other situation when you need to deal with distressing emotions of any kind. It consists of five steps:

  1. Stop
  2. Breathe
  3. Notice
  4. Reflect
  5. Respond

The first step is figuring out when you have been triggered. Some symptoms are: shallow breathing, sick to the stomach, experiencing a fight or flight response, having an emotional outburst, feeling like a victim and difficulty paying attention.

When you feel triggered, stop a moment and do nothing. Take several deep, conscious breaths, calming yourself. Notice how this emotion makes you feel. Ask yourself "Where is this emotion coming from?" Only then respond.

It is a very good technique to deal with things that would normally just set you off screaming. By simply stopping a moment before you respond, you can prevent situations from becoming bad.

You'll get better with practice.

If nothing else, taking this little pause will help you get a handle on your triggers and as a result help you manage control your emotional triggers with skill.
